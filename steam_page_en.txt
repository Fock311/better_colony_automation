[h2]❌ Still manually managing dozens of planets?[/h2]

Mid to late game in Stellaris often turns into a micromanagement nightmare:

[b]·[/b] Manually building on every single planet  
[b]·[/b] Vanilla automation building randomly and even ruining your economy  
[b]·[/b] Planet specialization requires tedious manual demolition and rebuilding  

As your empire grows to dozens or even hundreds of planets,  
this quickly becomes overwhelming.

[b]Better Colony Automation[/b] aims to solve this:

[b]Eliminate repetitive actions while keeping full control over your empire’s development.[/b]

[h2]✅ What does this mod do?[/h2]

✔ Automated construction [b]based on actual needs[/b] (not random building)  
✔ Automatically removes unsuitable buildings, districts, and specializations for smooth planet transitions  
✔ Smart resource control to prevent automation from crashing your economy  
✔ Highly configurable, but works out-of-the-box  
✔ Intuitive UI for planning and adjustments  

This mod is designed to [b]reduce workload, not replace player decisions.[/b]  
👉 [b]You set the direction — the system handles the execution.[/b]

[h2]Quick Guide[/h2]

1. [b]Enable automation[/b]: Turn on Planet Automation and configure options in the planet interface  
2. [b]Planning & district setup[/b]: Use the new UI to configure mixed planning and district construction  
3. [b]Global settings[/b]: Configure global behavior in Policies (e.g. mineral reserve for auto-construction)

This mod improves almost all vanilla automation options, including pop assembly, housing, rare resources, crime management, etc. You can safely leave these options on by default.

[h2]⚠️ Compatibility[/h2]

[b]It is strongly recommended to place this mod at the end of your load order[/b]

[b]·[/b] This mod primarily modifies vanilla automation logic and should not conflict with mods that do not touch automation  
[b]·[/b] Only supports vanilla buildings and districts (no support for modded buildings/districts for now)  
[b]·[/b] Not compatible with mods that add additional district specializations or new district types  
[b]·[/b] Compatible with mods that increase building slots  

This mod is compatible with Ultimate Automation,  
but [b]must be loaded after it[/b] to override its changes.
